Towards a Data-Driven Organisation

1. Background and Purpose

Brim Explorer and Brim Tech currently operate with data spread across multiple systems — accounting systems, booking platforms, HR tools, operational logs, and shipbuilding project data. The insight that exists is largely produced manually and is not available in real time.

This has three concrete consequences:

  • Decisions are made on the basis of outdated or incomplete information
  • Resources are spent on manual aggregation rather than analysis
  • Patterns and trends are discovered too late to act on

The purpose of this initiative is to address this — gradually and in a controlled manner — by building insight capacity where it delivers the greatest business value.

This is a joint initiative between Brim Explorer and Brim Tech. Business data belongs primarily to Brim Explorer (passengers, routes, finance, HR), while vessel data spans both companies (construction at Brim Tech, operations and maintenance at Brim Explorer). Collaboration and a shared data foundation are therefore a prerequisite.

2. What Does It Mean to Be Data-Driven?

A data-driven organisation produces insight of sufficient quality for use in decision-making processes. It is not primarily about technology — it is about understanding your own business better.

In practice, this means three things:

  1. Decision support — managers and team leads have access to relevant insight when they need it, without having to request manual data exports.
  2. Automated consolidation — reports and overviews that are currently produced manually are generated automatically and consistently.
  3. Access to history — the ability to identify patterns over time, not just point-in-time snapshots.

The path there is not straightforward. Early on, the work is about verifying that data exists, that it is reliable, and that it actually reflects reality. More advanced analysis and cross-system decision support comes after this foundation is in place.

3. Two Parallel Tracks

The initiative is structured into two tracks with different data logic, different systems, and different decision-makers — but with shared infrastructure and a shared methodology.

Track 1 — Business Analytics (Brim Explorer)

This track covers the commercial and administrative core of Brim Explorer.

Domain Examples of insight Business Value
Sales & Marketing Passenger volumes per route and season, price sensitivity, customer categories, route occupancy Better capacity utilisation, foundation for pricing strategy, identification of growth segments
Accounting & Finance Cost development per route/vessel, variance against budget, liquidity forecasts, revenue per passenger Faster detection of cost overruns, improved liquidity management, accurate budget forecasts
HR & Staffing Staffing overview, absence patterns, competency requirements, turnover, certificate expiry Reduced sick leave and turnover, better staffing planning, avoid competency gaps
Track 2 — Vessel Analytics (Brim Tech / Brim Explorer)

This track covers the entire vessel lifecycle — from construction to operations to maintenance. It is a shared responsibility between Brim Tech (construction and technology) and Brim Explorer (operations and maintenance).

Domain Examples of insight Business Value
Vessel Construction Build status per milestone, cost vs. budget, supplier performance, defect handling, hours consumed Earlier detection of delays and overruns, improved supplier management, documented build process
Operations Operating time per vessel, energy consumption per route and season, deviation from schedule, charging logs Early identification of operational anomalies, optimise routes and charging logistics, reduce energy costs
Maintenance Maintenance history per component, fault logs, time spent, costs, predictive patterns Transition from reactive to planned maintenance, reduced downtime, lower maintenance costs

The Vessel Track is particularly strategic for Brim Tech: structured operational and maintenance data is a competitive advantage in tenders and in the development of the Vessel Lifecycle Orchestration platform.

4. What This Strategy Can Deliver

The initiative is designed to deliver value along two dimensions:

Cost Reduction

  • Finance: Faster anomaly detection means faster correction. Automated reports free up time spent on manual extracts.
  • Operations: Energy optimisation and reduced downtime deliver direct cost savings per vessel.
  • Maintenance: Planned maintenance is cheaper than reactive maintenance. Operational data provides the basis for planning correctly.
  • HR: Better staffing planning reduces agency costs and overtime.

Foundation for Growth

  • Market: Insight into route occupancy and customer behaviour provides a basis for expanding and optimising the route offering.
  • Vessel Construction: Structured build data gives Brim Tech a documentation foundation for tenders and scaling to external customers.
  • Vessel Lifecycle Orchestration: Vessel data from operations and maintenance is the very core of Brim Tech's future service platform — and value accumulates from day one.

5. Approach and Phases

We recommend a phased approach that minimises risk and delivers visible value early:

Phase Content Duration Deliverable
1. Mapping Identify what data exists, where it resides, and what quality challenges exist. Interviews with 3–5 internal stakeholders per track to define concrete needs and prioritised use cases. 2–4 weeks Data map, prioritised use case list per track
2. Pilot Build one simple, functioning insight solution per track — based on the highest-priority use case. Verify that data and logic align with reality. 4–8 weeks Two functioning pilot solutions (one per track)
3. Expansion Following a successful pilot: gradual expansion to the remaining domains in each track. Establish routines for maintaining and updating solutions. Ongoing Insight capacity across all five domains
4. Advanced Analytics When the data foundation is solid: cross-domain analysis, predictive models, and integration with Brim Tech's Vessel Lifecycle Orchestration platform. From 2027 Predictive maintenance, cross-system insight, platform integration

Tentative timeline: Phase 1 begins autumn 2026. Phase 2 (pilot) is expected to run from late autumn 2026 into early 2027.

6. Key Success Factors

Experience from similar initiatives shows that the technical solution is rarely the limiting factor. What matters is:

  • Clear ownership — someone in each track who is accountable for the initiative and takes responsibility for data quality
  • Leadership involvement — the use cases must come from leaders who actually use the insight in their work
  • Realistic expectations — data quality issues will be discovered, and fixing them takes time
  • Small, working solutions — one functioning dashboard is worth more than an ambitious system that never gets deployed
  • Interface between companies — a formal data-sharing agreement between Brim Explorer and Brim Tech is required before the Vessel Track can be fully activated

7. Decisions — Leadership Responsibility

The following decisions rest with company leadership. The Board is informed of the direction taken.

  1. Strategic direction: Leadership confirms this initiative as a joint strategic priority for Brim Explorer and Brim Tech.
  2. Resources: Leadership allocates budget and capacity for the mapping phase and pilot.
  3. Ownership: Leadership designates an executive sponsor in each company and an operational owner per track.
  4. Inter-company interface: Leadership defines how data sharing and cost allocation between Brim Explorer and Brim Tech are governed.

8. What This Is Not

  • A single large ERP replacement or system procurement
  • A project that delivers everything at once — it is an iterative process over time
  • Purely a technology project — it requires business ownership and engagement from leaders
  • AI or advanced machine learning from day one — that comes after the data foundation is in place

9. Leadership Decision

Leadership has decided to:

  1. Launch the data analytics initiative as a joint strategic priority for Brim Explorer and Brim Tech
  2. Allocate a budget frame for Phase 1 (Mapping) and Phase 2 (Pilot) — with a checkpoint before Phase 3
  3. Designate an executive sponsor in each company with overall responsibility for the initiative
  4. Establish agreements on data sharing and cost allocation between the companies for the Vessel Track

The Board is briefed on this direction for information.
